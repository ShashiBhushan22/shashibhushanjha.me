About Me

Seeking opportunities in Wireless Systems Engineering, Signal Processing & PHY Algorithm Development

I am a Master of Technology student at IIT Ropar, specializing in Signal Processing and Communication, with current work centered on wireless systems, receiver design, and data-driven analysis for communication problems.

My master's thesis focuses on 5G uplink NOMA receiver design, where I study successive interference cancellation, BER behavior in Rayleigh fading channels, and machine learning assisted detection. Through projects in adaptive filtering, echo cancellation, and signal analysis, I am building a strong foundation in DSP, estimation, and algorithm evaluation.

I work comfortably with MATLAB, Python, C++, and Linux, and I use Telecom Demystified to sharpen how I communicate complex wireless concepts. My WIPO certifications remain a useful complement, but my current career focus is firmly on wireless communications and modem-oriented systems engineering.
